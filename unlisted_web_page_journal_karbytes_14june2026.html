In terms of what seems to be most realistic (rather than considering all the possibilities that are at least imaginable), the “worst case scenarios” for how my life could become are either being subject to extreme physical suffering which takes up so much of my life that I genuinely feel suicidal or, not quite as bad as that, being imprisoned by a society or institutions within it which prevent me from living autonomously (e.g. being incarcerated in a prison for “too much” of my lifespan) and from being able to (a) continue expanding and reviewing KARBYTES, (b) traveling alone in wilderness regions, (c) paying for products and services which are essential to expanding and joyfully reviewing KARBYTES.

If some variation of what I call “worst case scenario” happens to me, then I would say that KARBYTES is probably not going to continue being expanded. My hope and expecation is that, whether or not “worst case scenario” happens, KARBYTES will remain on the public World Wide Web for as long as the World Wide Web exists. (The “worst case scenario” category of situations generally includes the condition of me not being able to personally determine whether or not KARBYTES is still on the public World Wide Web and in an untampered with state. If “worst case scenario” happens to me, then I will most likely have to relinquish control and investment in KARBYTES and, instead, simply endure what remains of my hellish existence).

Rather than treat “worst case scenario” like something I expect to happen, I will simply acknowledge it as something I think could realistically happen while assuming that “worst case scenario” won’t actually happen. That way I can focus my attention on what I listed in the first paragraph of the body of this journal entry as (a), (b), and (c) with minimal distress and maximum agency.

I will assume that I will be able to do what I said in the previous paragraph if and only if I am sufficiently pro-social (i.e. not a threat to other people’s livelihoods and well-being and also a contributor to society in the form of some kind of paid occupation). I doubt I will be long-term unemployed following the year 2026 and I doubt I will legitimately qualify for state-funded disability payments. Hence, a major essential component to fueling the KARBYTES engine is having a stable adequately paying job.

In order for me to get such a job, I need to be competitve against other job applicants for the same role. Whether or not I like people or not, I feel obliged (for this end) to at least feign sociability, humbleness, and empathy. I am totally willing to do that also because even “faking it” seems to lift my mood and other people’s moods as well. In an era where human jobs are becoming increasingly outsourced to artificial intelligence (up to a certain point), the human jobs which do remain seem to be about providing what artificial intelligence alone cannot: human interfacing which results in positive experiences for all parties.

Another idea which has been on my mind lately is how my ability to practice what I conceptualize as “mindfulness meditation” seems severely hindered by the “psychotronic harassment” I have mentioned repeatedly throughout KARBYTES (because the “psychotronic harassment” manifests to me in the form of intrusive and hostile human voices or mouth sounds which almost always include verbal content and which seem to be immediately responding to what I am perceiving through my senses and to what I am thinking about (especially thoughts containing verbal content)). I thought that perhaps practicing “mindfulness meditation” for me (especially while experiencing such “psychotronic harassment”) is mostly a waste of my time and amounts to doing little other than focusing on distressing and inescapable “psychotronic harasment” and too little focus on qualia that I would focus my attention on more fully in the absence of such “psychotronic harassment”. Also, I thought that perhaps doing “the right kinds of” psychedelics (especially 5-MeO-DMT and, to a lesser extent, psilocybin and N,N-Dimethyltryptamine (DMT)) accomplishes what “mindfulness medication” is allegedly supposed to accomplish with orders of magnitude more efficiency.

I have been interested in “mindfulness meditation” and “modern spirituality” since I was a teenager and even attended daylong mindfulness meditation retreats at places like Spirit Rock Meditation Center in Marin County, California and East Bay Meditation Center in Oakland, California. I felt embarassed every time I went because I seemed a bit like a social outlier compared to the other people attending those meetups. I’m glad I went though because I was intrigued by those places (especially Spirit Rock) by what I saw online. Maybe someday I will attend another daylong just for the fun of it when the timing seems appropriate. Until then, I would like to seriously get into some kind of sustainable mindfulness practice in conjunction with immersive hikes (and sometimes camping) in The Big Green Thing (and other wilderness destinations) and strategic use of psychedelics. I think that I may be able to get into a formal sitting/lying down mindfulness meditation practice if I don’t overly strain for a particular outcome and, instead, treat each session as a “timeout” from being more engaged in more overt decision-making activity and analysis; a deliberately carved-out allocation of space-time (and mass-energy) dedicated to allowing the organism I am to appreciate its own existence with minimal coercion and with maximal receptivity to “cosmic intelligence”. That way, I can re-contextualize such “formal” meditation sessions as encompassing the “psychotronic harassment” and my emotional, conceptual, and physiological responses to such harassment in a maximally impersonal and non-judgemental manner; like a scientist coldly studying specimens undergoing changes in a petri dish under a microscope instead of feeling more literally identified with the agenda and feelings of the specimen being studied (and perhaps desecrated while being studied). I like the idea of observing reality from the localized aperture of reality that I am while identifying with reality (i.e. nature) itself instead of as that (likely temporary and transient) aperture.

Based on what I currently know about reality, the aperture I apparently am is expected to eventually permanently close or vanish entirely from all subjective fields of awareness. All that might ever remain of that aperture (and the memories and actions which were implemented through that aperture) could at least hypothetially be forever preserved in some realm of nature beyond the scope of time; perhaps in some purely noumenal space or perhaps even in some kind of special immutable library that can literally be visited by some types of information processing agents. I hope to become such an information processing agent and I hope that such an information processing agent is also immortal.

The previous paragraph reminds me of what I was thinking about writing down yesterday but decided not to (until today) because those thoughts seemed redundant and overly simplistic rather than more novel and intellectually rigorous and complex. What I was thinking about writing down yesterday is (approximately) as follows:

In all situations, nature is all I have and all I am.
Nature ought to be the place I go to for refuge and renewal especially in times of distress.
Perhaps returning to nature in adverserial situations is akin to reaching the case case of a recursive function rather than deepening the adversity by yet another layer of recursion.
