Last year I created the following web page:

https://karbytesforlifeblog.wordpress.com/radical_forgiveness_spell_card_journal_entry/

The purpose of that web page was/is to provide a tangible reminder to avoid holding grudges and, instead, minimizing how much resentment is preserved for the sake of maximizing happiness.

Today I want to create a follow-up to that web page which takes the form of this journal entry.

The purpose of this journal entry is to provide a logical basis for why one should forgive past transgressions and so-called imperfections.

The logical basis for minimizing grudges I am presenting here is the idea that all information processing agents (especially human individuals and human civilization) “started off” from more primitive and less structurally robust configurations which made civilized, ethical behavior harder for them to implement than when those entities acquired more sophisticated configurations (and that such primitive configurations are inextricably part of those entities’ evolution and, more generally, part of the overal evolution and web of causality comprising those entities’ encompassing universe/multiverse.

An example of a naive moral framework is one which categorizes a person as irredeemably unworthy of happiness or continued existence on the basis of that person ever having committed a serious enough moral violation. Such a moral framework would only allow “morally perfect” people to fully participate in society rather than be discarded. My reasoning is that even the most so-called “morally perfect” people came into existence in conjunction with (and as a result of) prior “moral imperfections” such as the fact that their ancestors almost certainly committed heinous or merely callous acts of cruelty for the sake of surviving in a ruthlessly competitive environment (especially in times before those animals had access to modern conveniences which make such savagery unnecessary).

My reasoning is predicated on the assumption that all persons, events, and objects are immutably part of the same all-encompassing causal or generative web referred to as nature. Rejecting “unsavory” components of nature is rejecting nature as a whole (and my idea for a sane and compassionate society is one which is based on a holistic and scientifically-informed worldview rather than on localized emotional appeal or cultural trends).

This is not to say that “dangerous” people (or other threats) should not be quarantined away from everyone else whenever possible to prevent harm, but I think that the society I live in still tends to promote retribution instead of simply minimizing every person’s suffering (including the suffering of the worst criminal offenders).

In a nutshell, I think that all moral frameworks “should” boil down to the following essential precept: minimize suffering everywhere and maximize happiness everywhere.
